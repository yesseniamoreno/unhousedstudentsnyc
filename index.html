How Remote Learning Affects Unhoused Children in NYC

In the 2018-2019 school year, 114,000 New York City public school students met the definition of homelessness: Lacking a fixed, regular and adequate nighttime residence.

Begining with drastic cuts made to public housing during the Regan era, family homelessness has increased drastically in the last four decades. In the last decade alone, the number of homeless students in New York City has increased by 70 percent.

Of the 1.1 million public school stundents in NYC, 42% are enrolled in all-remote learning; however 100% of students will need access to technogloy and widi in order to fully partiiate in their education.

Statistics in green are for housed students while those in red are for unhoused students.

Housed vs Unhoused Students in New York City

A Consisent Home

1 in 10 children in NYC are homeless. NYC's eviction moratorium-- State Tenant's Safe Harbor Act-- will expire on on January 1, 2021. The sudden loss of housing protections for familes experiencing financial difficulty due to the pandemic, may futher increase the number of homeless children in NYC.

By Race

In 2019, nearly 85 percent of NYC homeless students were Black or Latinx. White students and "Other" made up the remaining 15%.

Access to WIFI

Over 100,000 students living in shelters or unstable housing in NYC lack access to regular WIFI or necessary technology for remote learning. Meanwhile, 230,000 of low-income households (earning below $20,000 annually) are without internet, as well.

Graduation Rates

Currently, lacking a High School diploma is the single most determiner of adult homelessness, nationwide.
